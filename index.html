Habitat loss of threatened Avian Animals in Australia

What are Avian Animals

  • They are move well known as 'birds'
  • Vertebrate animals adapted with the ability to fly
  • Based on the Australian Threatened Species Index for Bird by the Threatened Species Index Organisation (TSX), it is evident that there have been a decrease in population of threatened Avian animals throughout the years.
  • From the year 2012, the population starts to decrease at an even faster rate with the decrease being the most evident and drastic as it progress from 2018 as seen from the steepness of the slope.

The reason for this was due to the rate of destruction of threatened species habitat approved by the Australian government.

‘The rate of destruction is increasing. During the ﬁve year period from 2011 to 2016 the government approved the destruction of 80,000 hectares of threatened species habitat. Despite the worsening trajectory for threatened species outlined in the 2016. State of the Environment Report, the rate of habitat destruction accelerated by 50% in the following ﬁve years. Between 2016 and 2021, more than 120,000 hectares of threatened species habitat was approved for destruction’

It dealt the greatest damage to Avian animals as compared to other animals with numerous species of threatened Avian animals habitat being badly impacted as a result of the destruction.

Based on the dataset complied by the Australian Conservation Foundation(ACF) over the destruction of threatened species habitat across the span of 10 years from 2012 to 2021, half of the top 20 threatened species impacted were Avian animals

Fiqure 1 illustrates the compilation of the dataset into a pie-chart, showcasing the area of hectares that were approved to be destroyed during the time period against the top 20 threatened species.

Habitats of avian animals were largely impacted as a total of 56,327 hectares of habitat were destroyed, equivalent to 1/4 of Adelaide’s entire metropolitan zone. It also accounts for 46 % of the total hectares approved to be destroyed from 2012 to 202
